Key Parties & Roles
🏛️
Trustee

SEBI-registered, independent trustee (e.g. Axis Trustee). Holds legal title to all assets on behalf of unitholders. Cannot be related to sponsor.

►Legal owner of all REIT assets
►Ensures SEBI compliance
►Approves major transactions
►Cannot be sponsor-affiliated
🏗️
Sponsor

The developer/asset owner who creates the REIT (e.g. Embassy Group, Blackstone, K Raheja Corp). Must retain minimum 15% stake for 3 years.

►Seeds the REIT with initial assets
►Must hold min 15% units (3yr lock-in)
►Provides pipeline for future acquisitions
►Gets ROFO on new assets (usually)
⚙️
Manager

The investment manager that runs day-to-day operations, makes investment decisions, and manages properties. Earns management fee (~0.5-1% of AUM).

►Day-to-day investment decisions
►Leasing, tenant management
►Reports to board + unitholders
►Earns fee — aligns with NDCF growth
🔍
Valuer

SEBI-mandated independent property valuer (e.g. Knight Frank, JLL). Values all assets minimum twice a year. Critical for NAV calculation.

►Semi-annual independent valuation
►Determines NAV per unit
►Audited by two separate valuers
►Valuation drives price discovery
📋
SEBI

Securities and Exchange Board of India regulates all Indian REITs under the SEBI (REIT) Regulations 2014.

►Approves REIT registration
►Sets leverage limits (49% of AUM)
►Mandates 90% NDCF distribution
►Unitholder consent for major acts
🏢
SPV

Special Purpose Vehicles — individual companies that hold each property asset. The REIT holds ≥50% in each SPV. SPVs are the actual landlords.

►Individual asset ownership entity
►REIT holds ≥50% in each SPV
►Generates rental income at SPV level
►NDCF flows up from SPV to REIT

How Distributions Are Taxed
💰
Interest Income
Taxable in investor hands at slab rate
Largest component. REIT/InvIT pays TDS at 10% for residents. Most distributions are classified here.
📊
Dividend Income
Taxable at investor's slab rate + TDS 10%
Distributed from SPV dividends. TDS deducted at source. Same treatment as equity dividends.
📈
Capital Gains
STCG 20% (< 12 months) · LTCG 12.5% (> 12 months)
On unit sale in secondary market. Same as listed equity. 12-month holding for LTCG.
🔄
Return of Capital
Tax-free in investor hands
Repayment of principal. Reduces cost of acquisition — taxed as capital gain on eventual unit sale. Most tax-efficient component.
⚠️ Tax Note: Each quarterly distribution from a REIT/InvIT comes with a breakdown of interest, dividend, and return of capital components. The trust issues a certificate showing the split. Tax treatment applies component-wise — not uniformly on the full DPU.
Why NDCF ≠ EBITDA — The Most Misunderstood Concept
"The distribution yield is 7% based on EBITDA."
❌ This is wrong. Here's why:
EBITDA ignores three major cash outflows that must come before distributions reach unitholders:
1
Scheduled Amortisation
Principal repayment is a real cash outflow. A REIT with ₹400 Cr EBITDA and ₹300 Cr annual amortisation has only ₹100 Cr left for distribution — not ₹400 Cr.
2
Maintenance Capex
Assets need ongoing investment to maintain condition and lease-ability. EBITDA adds back depreciation — but depreciation exists for a reason. Maintenance capex is the cash version.
3
Working Capital
Security deposits received, advance rent, or WC movements all affect real cash available. EBITDA ignores these entirely.
✓ Always use NDCF — not EBITDA — to evaluate REIT/InvIT distributions.

InvIT Asset Classes & Revenue Models
🛣️
Highway — Toll
9–12%
Revenue: Vehicle users pay per trip. Annual WPI-linked revision.
Key Risk: Traffic volume risk. Economic slowdown = lower toll revenue.
e.g. Cube Highways, Raajmarg InvIT (toll assets)
🏛️
Highway — Annuity
7–9%
Revenue: Govt pays fixed semi-annual annuity regardless of traffic.
Key Risk: Near-zero — counterparty is NHAI (Govt of India).
e.g. NHIT InvIT, Raajmarg (annuity assets)
⚡
Power Transmission
8–10%
Revenue: CERC/SERC tariff based on grid availability (not power volume). 99%+ availability = full tariff.
Key Risk: Near-zero demand risk. Regulatory risk (tariff revision every 5 yrs).
e.g. IndiGrid, PowerGrid InvIT
☀️
Renewable Energy
7–9%
Revenue: Long-term PPAs (25 yrs) with state discoms or central agencies at fixed tariff.
Key Risk: Discom payment delays. Weather/PLF risk.
e.g. SEIT (Mahindra Susten)
🔥
Gas Pipeline
15–20%
Revenue: PNGRB regulated tariff + contracted capacity payments (RIL PUA for EIT).
Key Risk: Volume risk below contracted minimum. Tariff dispute risk.
e.g. Energy Infrastructure Trust (EIT)
📡
Telecom Towers
8–11%
Revenue: Monthly lease rental per tenancy from telco operators (Jio, Airtel, Vi).
Key Risk: Tenancy loss if Vi fails. Renewal risk at lease expiry.
e.g. Altius Telecom InvIT, Vertis
📦
Warehousing / Logistics
8–10%
Revenue: Long-term leases (5–15 yrs) with 3PL, e-commerce, FMCG tenants. Annual escalations.
Key Risk: Occupancy risk. E-commerce demand concentration.
e.g. NDR InvIT Trust
